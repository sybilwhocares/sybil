Night Train — New Delhi to Srinagar

Departure evening · Overnight journey · Arrives next morning

▾
  • 3:00 PM
    Reach New Delhi Railway Station
    Head to New Delhi (NDLS) station by 5 PM. Platform 1–5 typically for Vande Bharat trains. Grab dinner at one of the station restaurants or Karim's nearby.
  • 7:00 PM
    Board Vande Bharat Sleeper
    Train departs sharp. Find your berths, settle in. The train passes through Haryana, Punjab plains — watch the city lights fade as you roll north.
  • Overnight
    Sleep through the Shivaliks
    Around 2:30 AM you pass Jammu Tawi. The train enters the dramatic Udhampur-Srinagar-Baramulla rail link with tunnels through the Himalayas. Sleep well — big day ahead.

Srinagar — Dal Lake & the Old City

Arrive 8 AM · Shikara rides · Mughal Gardens · Houseboat night

▾
  • 8:00 AM
    Arrive Srinagar Station
    Your rich friend's driver meets you at the station with the Innova Crysta. Freshen up, check into houseboat on Dal Lake. These floating palaces are a Srinagar icon — cedar-panelled, with plush carpets and a personal cook.
  • 10:00 AM
    Shikara Ride on Dal Lake
    Board a traditional Shikara and glide through the 26 sq km lake. Pass floating vegetable gardens, lotus beds, and floating markets. Watch fishermen cast nets in the morning mist. About 2 hours. Cost: ₹600–800 for the whole boat.
  • 12:30 PM
    Lunch at Mughal Darbar
    One of Srinagar's legendary Wazwan restaurants. Must-try: Rogan Josh, Yakhni, Gushtaba, Haak Saag. Lunch for 3: ₹1,800–2,500.
  • 2:00 PM
    Shalimar Bagh — Mughal Garden
    Built by Emperor Jahangir for Noor Jahan in 1619, these terraced Mughal gardens rise toward the hills with cascading fountains and chinar trees. Entry: ₹24/person.
  • 3:30 PM
    Nishat Bagh (Garden of Bliss)
    Largest Mughal garden in Kashmir, designed in 1633. Twelve terraces represent 12 zodiac signs. Stunning Dal Lake backdrop. Entry: ₹24/person.
  • 5:00 PM
    Hazratbal Shrine
    The white marble domed mosque on Dal Lake's north shore is one of Kashmir's most revered shrines. Dress respectfully. Free entry. Mesmerising at dusk with the lake reflections.
  • 7:00 PM
    Sunset on Dal Lake + Evening Shikara
    Back to the houseboat. Order Kahwa (saffron-cardamom tea) on the deck and watch the Zabarwan mountains turn pink and purple. The reflections of houseboats on the still lake at dusk are otherworldly.
  • 8:30 PM
    Dinner on the Houseboat
    Your houseboat cook prepares a Wazwan feast: Tabak Maaz (fried ribs), Seekh Kebabs, Dum Aloo, and Phirni for dessert. Usually included in houseboat tariff.
☕ Kahwa + Sheermal breakfast 🍖 Wazwan lunch 🍛 Houseboat dinner 🛶 Luxury Houseboat on Dal Lake ~0 km driving

Gulmarg — The Meadow of Flowers

56 km from Srinagar · World's highest gondola · Snow even in summer

▾
  • 7:00 AM
    Early departure to Gulmarg
    Drive 56 km (1.5 hrs) through apple and walnut orchards. The road winds through Tangmarg before the final ascent to Gulmarg at 2,650 m. Driver arranges roadside chai.
  • 9:00 AM
    Gulmarg Gondola — Phase 1 & 2
    The world's highest operating cable car (3,980 m at Phase 2 Apharwat peak). Phase 1 to Kongdori: ₹740/person. Phase 2 to Apharwat: ₹1,550/person. Up top: 360° Himalayan panorama, snow in July, and a little tea shack at 13,000 ft!
  • 12:30 PM
    Horse riding on the meadow
    Gulmarg's vast flat meadow (bowl) is perfect for horse rides. Local ponies circle the meadow past wildflowers and snow patches. Rate: ₹400–600 per person per hour. Bargain confidently.
  • 2:00 PM
    Lunch at Highlands Park or Hotel Affarwat
    Try the Kashmiri Trout — caught fresh from mountain streams, cooked in a mustard-based gravy. With bread, chai, and dessert for 3: ₹1,500–2,000.
  • 3:30 PM
    St. Mary's Church & Golf Course
    The 1895 British-era stone church sits in a meadow below the slopes. Right next to it is the world's highest green golf course — bring a club for a novelty round (rentals available, ₹800).
  • 5:30 PM
    Drive back to Srinagar
    Return via Tangmarg. Stop at a local farm for fresh apples and walnuts. Back in Srinagar by 7:30 PM.
  • 8:00 PM
    Dinner at Ahdoos Hotel
    Srinagar's most famous restaurant since 1918. Famous for their Seekh Kebabs and Kashmiri Dum Aloo. Evening at Lal Chowk market for Pashmina shopping. Budget: ₹500–800/person for dinner.
🧇 Houseboat breakfast 🐟 Kashmiri Trout lunch 🍖 Kebabs at Ahdoos 🛶 Houseboat on Dal Lake (2nd night) ~112 km round trip
3

Sonamarg → Zoji La → Kargil

Srinagar to Kargil: 205 km · Cross the mighty Zoji La Pass

▾
  • 6:30 AM
    Checkout & depart Srinagar
    Early start is crucial — Zoji La can get traffic. Pack a snack bag. Your driver loads the Innova and you leave the houseboat behind. The road follows the Sindh River northeast.
  • 9:00 AM
    Sonamarg — Meadow of Gold
    At 2,730 m, Sonamarg is one of Kashmir's crown jewels. The wide green valley flanked by snow peaks is staggering. Take a pony ride to Thajiwas Glacier (mandatory — SUVs can't go beyond the base). Cost: ₹600–800/person for pony + guide. Spend 2 hours here.
  • 11:30 AM
    Crossing Zoji La Pass (3,528 m)
    The 'gateway to Ladakh'. Zoji La literally means "mountain pass of blizzards". The road becomes narrow, rocky, and dramatic — a vertiginous 11 km climb. Keep your eyes open and your phone charged for photos. Your driver has crossed this hundreds of times.
  • 12:30 PM
    Drass — World's 2nd Coldest Inhabited Place
    Lunch stop at Drass. At 3,280 m, Drass sees -60°C in winter! The summer day feels merely brisk. Have daal, rice, and chapati at a dhaba. Cost: ₹150–200/person.
  • 2:30 PM
    Kargil War Memorial
    An emotional visit. The 1999 Kargil War saw fierce fighting on these peaks between India and Pakistan. The memorial honours the 527 Indian soldiers who fell. The sound-and-light show (evenings) is deeply moving. Free entry.
  • 4:00 PM
    Tiger Hill viewpoint
    Tactical hill from the 1999 war. The views of snow-capped peaks stretching to the horizon are extraordinary.
  • 6:00 PM
    Arrive Kargil & Check In
    Kargil city straddles the Suru River surrounded by bare brown mountains. Check into Hotel Siachen or Hotel Greenland (₹2,500–4,000/night for a double). The town has a unique Shia Muslim culture — visit the bazaar for local apricots and butter tea.
  • 8:00 PM
    Dinner & Kargil Night Market
    Try Ladakhi Thukpa (noodle soup) or Skyu (thick pasta with root vegetables). Very hearty for cold mountain nights.
☕ Houseboat breakfast 🍲 Dhaba lunch in Drass 🍜 Thukpa dinner in Kargil 🏨 Hotel in Kargil ~205 km
4

Kargil → Lamayuru → Leh

230 km · Moonland Monastery · Magnetic Hill · Leh arrival

▾
  • 7:00 AM
    Departure from Kargil
    Long day of driving — 230 km on winding mountain roads takes 7–8 hours with stops. Pack breakfast from the hotel. The landscape transforms dramatically as you leave the lush Suru Valley.
  • 9:00 AM
    Mulbekh Monastery & Giant Buddha
    A 9-metre carved Buddha (circa 8th century AD) is chiselled directly into a rock face beside the road — one of Ladakh's oldest Buddhist monuments. 15-minute stop.
  • 10:30 AM
    Lamayuru Monastery — Moonland
    Ladakh's oldest and largest monastery perches above a surreal eroded landscape that looks lunar — hence "Moonland". Founded in the 10th century, it houses over 150 monks and priceless thangka paintings. Explore for 1–1.5 hours. Entry: ₹50/person.
  • 12:30 PM
    Lunch at Alchi or roadside dhaba
    The road passes Alchi village with its 1,000-year-old murals. Lunch at a local dhaba: Ladakhi red rice with lentils and pickled vegetables. ₹200–300/person.
  • 2:30 PM
    Confluence of Indus & Zanskar Rivers
    One of Ladakh's most photographed spots. The turquoise Zanskar meets the darker Indus in a dramatic confluence visible from a viewpoint above. The distinct colour difference of the two rivers is striking. Free, 20 minutes.
  • 3:30 PM
    Magnetic Hill
    An optical illusion where vehicles appear to roll uphill on their own. Put your SUV in neutral at the marked spot and watch! The explanation is a trick of perspective in the surrounding terrain — fun regardless. Free.
  • 4:00 PM
    Gurudwara Pathar Sahib
    Built in 1517 and maintained by the Indian Army, this Sikh shrine sits beside the NH1. Accept langar (free community meal) from the soldiers — a moving experience of faith and military brotherhood.
  • 5:30 PM
    Hall of Fame Museum
    Built by the Indian Army to honour martyrs of the Siachen and Kargil conflicts. Weapons, uniforms, documents. Very moving. Entry: ₹50/person. 45 minutes.
  • 7:00 PM
    Arrive Leh & Check In
    Check into The Grand Dragon Ladakh or Jigmet Guest House. REST is critical tonight — acclimatize to 3,500 m altitude. No alcohol. Drink lots of water. Slow down.
🧇 Hotel breakfast 🍚 Ladakhi dhaba lunch 🫕 Army langar + hotel dinner 🏨 Hotel in Leh (rest & acclimatize) ~230 km
5

Leh City Exploration + Permits

Leh Palace · Shanti Stupa · Leh Market · Get Inner Line Permits

▾
⚠️ Acclimatization day! Move slowly, drink 4+ litres of water, avoid alcohol. Altitude sickness (AMS) can ruin the trip. Signs: headache, nausea, dizziness. Rest if symptoms appear.
  • 8:00 AM
    Inner Line Permits at DC Office
    Essential first task. The DC Office near the main bazaar issues ILPs for Nubra Valley, Pangong Tso, and Hanle. Bring 3 passport photos and ID copies each. Apply early (opens 9 AM) to avoid queues. Online option also available. Cost: ₹200–400/person per permit area. Takes 1–2 hours.
  • 10:30 AM
    Leh Palace (Lhachen Palkhar)
    Built in the 1600s by King Sengge Namgyal, this 9-storey palace dominates the hilltop. It predates the Potala Palace in Lhasa! The views over Leh city, the Indus Valley, and the Stok Kangri range are breathtaking. Entry: ₹100/person. 1.5 hours.
  • 12:30 PM
    Lunch at Bon Appetit or The Tibetan Kitchen
    Leh has surprisingly cosmopolitan cafés. Try Tibetan Momos (dumplings) with chilli sauce, Thukpa noodle soup, or yak cheese pizza at the expat favourites. Budget: ₹400–600/person.
  • 2:00 PM
    Namgyal Tsemo Gompa
    The monastery at the very top of the Namgyal Tsemo ridge above Leh Palace. Houses a giant Maitreya Buddha statue. A gentle hike up. Entry: ₹30.
  • 3:30 PM
    Leh Main Bazaar + Shopping
    The labyrinthine bazaar sells Pashmina shawls, Thangka paintings, turquoise jewellery, dried apricots, sea buckthorn juice, and prayer wheels. Budget ₹2,000–5,000 per person for souvenirs.
  • 5:00 PM
    Shanti Stupa — Sunset Ceremony
    The gleaming white Japanese-built stupa crowns a hilltop southwest of Leh at 3,694 m. 500 steps up or drive. The Dalai Lama consecrated it in 1985. Sunset here — with the stupa glowing and the mountains behind — is among Ladakh's finest moments. Free entry.
  • 7:30 PM
    Dinner at Gesmo Restaurant
    A Leh institution. Order the Yak Steak (if you eat red meat), Skyu pasta stew, and Chang (local barley beer — just one, easy on the altitude!). Budget: ₹500–800/person.
☕ Hotel breakfast 🥟 Momos & Thukpa lunch 🥩 Gesmo dinner 🏨 Hotel in Leh (2nd night) ~10 km city driving
6

Khardung La → Nubra Valley

World's highest motorable road · Bactrian camels · Hunder dunes

▾
  • 6:00 AM
    Early start — crucial for Khardung La
    Khardung La can close due to military convoys or weather. Leave Leh by 7 AM. It's 39 km to the pass — the road winds sharply up through multiple hairpin bends. Dress very warmly — it's well below freezing up top even in August.
  • 8:30 AM
    Khardung La Pass — 18,380 ft (5,602 m)
    The BRO (Border Roads Organisation) declared this the world's highest motorable road. At the top: a small tea stall run by the Indian Army (welcome cup of chai), giant signboards, prayer flags snapping in the icy wind, and views of the Saser Massif and Zanskar Range simultaneously. Spend 20–30 min max — altitude!
  • 10:30 AM
    Descend into Nubra Valley
    The descent from 5,600 m into the Nubra Valley (3,048 m) is jaw-dropping. The valley floor is lush and green — a hidden garden between brown Himalayan ridges. The Shyok and Nubra rivers converge here.
  • 12:00 PM
    Diskit Monastery — 500 years old
    The oldest and largest monastery in Nubra, perched on a rocky spur above the valley. The 32-metre Maitreya (Future Buddha) statue looks out protectively over the valley. The views from the monastery roof are spectacular. Entry: ₹50.
  • 2:00 PM
    Lunch in Diskit + Check-in at Sand Dune Camp
    Check into a luxury camp or guesthouse at Hunder — canvas tents with proper beds, solar-powered warm water. Budget: ₹3,500–6,000/tent per night.
  • 4:00 PM
    Bactrian Camel Safari — Hunder Sand Dunes
    A bizarre and magnificent sight: sand dunes in a high-altitude Himalayan valley, with double-humped Bactrian camels (descendants of the ancient Silk Road trade caravans). A 30-minute ride through the dunes is surreal. Cost: ₹400–600/person. The dunes glow amber at sunset.
  • 6:30 PM
    Sunset at Hunder Sand Dunes
    Nubra has the clearest skies on earth. As the sun sets behind the peaks, the valley turns golden. Sit on the dunes and watch. Bring a jacket — temperature drops fast.
  • 8:00 PM
    Bonfire & Stargazing
    Camp bonfires with local folk music, butter tea, and an absolutely staggering starscape overhead. No light pollution. The Milky Way is visible to the naked eye.
☕ Hotel breakfast 🍲 Dhaba lunch in Diskit 🍗 Camp dinner (included) ⛺ Luxury desert camp, Hunder ~125 km from Leh
7

Nubra → Pangong Tso → Fly Home

The 3 Idiots lake at 14,270 ft · Blue-to-turquoise-to-green magic

▾
Note: Nubra to Pangong via Shyok Valley is 5–6 hours (140 km). This route through the Shyok River valley is dramatically scenic but rough. Leave by 7 AM for a 1 PM arrival. Then catch a late afternoon/evening flight from Leh.
  • 5:30 AM
    Dawn at the dunes
    Wake up to sunrise over the Karakoram Range from your tent. The light hits the peaks at 6 AM. Worth setting an alarm for.
  • 7:00 AM
    Depart Nubra via Shyok Valley
    The route follows the Shyok River — a dirt track in places, with dramatic river crossings and canyon walls. Your driver navigates this expertly. Stop at Khalsar junction for chai.
  • 10:00 AM
    Durbuk village
    Half-way lunch stop. The road climbs out of the Shyok Valley. Durbuk has a small market and a military check post where ILPs are verified. Grab local apricot jam bread here.
  • 12:30 PM
    Pangong Tso — First Glimpse
    Nothing prepares you for it. You crest a ridge, and there it is: 134 km of shimmering lake at 4,350 m, stretching into Tibet (60% of the lake is in China). The water changes colour as you watch — cobalt, teal, sapphire, turquoise. Immortalised as the final scene of 3 Idiots.
  • 1:30 PM
    Lunch on the lakeshore
    Dozens of small restaurants and camps line the shore. Order Thukpa, Maggi (oddly perfect here), and yak butter tea while your feet dangle over the impossibly blue water. Budget: ₹300–400/person.
  • 3:00 PM
    Explore the shoreline & 3 Idiots spot
    Walk along the shore, skip stones, photograph the Himalayan peaks reflected in the lake. Visit the famous 3 Idiots movie location marked with a small sign. Rent bicycles or horses for ₹400–500 for a lakeside ride.
  • 4:30 PM
    Drive back to Leh (via Chang La)
    150 km via Chang La Pass (5,360 m) — the 3rd highest motorable road in the world. Stop at the top for photos with the prayer flags. Reach Leh by 7:30–8 PM.
  • 8:30 PM
    Final dinner in Leh
    Last meal together at Lehchen Restaurant or ALCHI Kitchen. Order everything you haven't tried yet. Share stories, look at photos, celebrate an incredible 7 days.
  • Next Morning
    Fly home from Leh Airport
    Leh's airport (Kushok Bakula Rimpochee) is served by IndiGo, SpiceJet, Air India, and Vistara. Flights to Delhi (~1.5 hrs): ₹3,500–7,000. Book well in advance. Check out by 7 AM for morning flights.
🧇 Camp breakfast 🍜 Lakeside Thukpa lunch 🍛 Final Leh dinner 🏨 Hotel Leh (last night) ~290 km total

Essential Tips

Everything you need to know to have the perfect trip — and nothing to ruin it.

🏔

Altitude sickness (AMS)

Rest on arrival in Leh. Drink 4+ litres of water daily. No alcohol first 24 hours. Diamox (acetazolamide) helps — consult a doctor before travel. Ascend gradually: Srinagar → Kargil → Leh is the perfect acclimatization route.

📱

SIM cards & connectivity

BSNL is the only telecom that works reliably in Ladakh. Buy a BSNL SIM before leaving Delhi or at Leh airport. No other provider (Jio/Airtel/Vi) works beyond Kargil. Download offline Google Maps for Ladakh.

💵

Cash is king

ATMs in Leh are scarce and often empty. Withdraw ₹20,000+ in Delhi before boarding the train. Kargil has 1–2 ATMs (often queued). Nubra and Pangong: zero ATMs. Card payments are rare beyond Leh city.

🌤

Best time to visit

June–September for the full route. July–August for Kashmir's wildflower blooms. September for fewer crowds. Avoid October–May: Zoji La closes, Khardung La can be treacherous, and temperatures plunge to -20°C at high passes.

🚗

Your SUV & driver

The Innova Crysta is the perfect vehicle for this terrain — tough, spacious, and high clearance. Trust your driver — they know the roads, passes, and checkpoints. Tip generously (₹1,000–2,000 total is nice). No overtaking at blind bends.

📄

Documents to carry

Carry 10+ photocopies of your ID (Aadhaar/Passport). Check posts verify IDs frequently. For Inner Line Permits: original ID + photo copies + passport photos. Keep digital scans on Google Drive too.

🧥

Layers are everything

Even in June, Khardung La is -5°C and Pangong is freezing at night. During the day in Srinagar it can be 25°C. Master the art of layering: base layer, fleece, windproof outer shell. Pack thermal underwear regardless of month.

🌞

Sun is brutal at altitude

At 4,000+ metres, UV radiation is extreme. SPF 50+ sunscreen, UV-protective sunglasses, and a wide-brimmed hat are non-negotiable. You can get severe sunburn in 20 minutes at Pangong or Khardung La even on a cloudy day.

🏥

Medical kit

Diamox, ibuprofen, ORS sachets, blister plasters, antiseptic cream, Diamox (125–250 mg/day, prophylactic for altitude). Leh has SNM Hospital and a few private clinics. Nearest major hospital is Chandigarh — 2+ days away by road.

⛺

Camp booking in Nubra

Book Desert Himalaya Camp or similar luxury camps at Hunder 2–3 months in advance for June–August. These fill up fast. WhatsApp is the easiest booking method. Cost: ₹3,500–6,000 per tent/night including dinner and breakfast.

🚂

Train booking tip

IRCTC opens bookings 60 days in advance. Log in at exactly 8 AM on the opening day for best selection. Tatkal quota opens at 10 AM, 1 day before journey. Consider booking a full coupe (4 berths in 2AC) for your group of 3 — more private.

🕌

Cultural respect

Remove shoes before entering any monastery or mosque. Ask before photographing locals. Don't touch prayer wheels with your left hand. Ladakhi and Kashmiri cultures both deserve respect — local etiquette is warmly appreciated and reciprocated.
